Case Study 01

FinFlow — Redesigning the Future of Business Banking

A complete UX overhaul of a B2B fintech platform that increased user activation by 47% and reduced onboarding drop-off by 62%.

RoleLead UX/UI Designer
Timeline6 months
Team2 Designers, 4 Engineers, 1 PM
IndustryFintech / B2B SaaS
FinFlow Dashboard — Final Design

The Challenge

FinFlow's legacy platform suffered from a convoluted onboarding flow and a dashboard built by engineers without UX oversight. 68% of trial users abandoned during setup, and the average time-to-value was 14 days — far too long for a competitive fintech market.

The company needed a ground-up redesign that would reduce friction, accelerate activation, and position them against emerging competitors like Mercury and Brex.

Goals

User Goals
  • Get set up in under 10 minutes
  • Understand the dashboard at a glance
  • Complete key tasks without training
  • Feel confident managing business finances
Business Goals
  • Increase trial-to-paid conversion by 30%
  • Reduce onboarding drop-off by 50%
  • Cut support tickets by 40%
  • Establish premium market positioning

Process

01
Research & Discovery

Conducted 24 user interviews, analyzed 3 months of analytics data, and mapped the existing user journey to identify pain points. Created empathy maps and personas for 3 primary user segments.

02
Competitive Benchmarking

Audited 8 competitors across fintech and SaaS. Mapped feature parity, onboarding patterns, and dashboard information architecture to identify differentiation opportunities.

03
Information Architecture

Restructured the navigation from 32 items to 8 primary categories. Ran card sorting sessions with 15 users to validate the new taxonomy and hierarchy.

04
Wireframing & Prototyping

Created low-fidelity wireframes for 40+ screens, iterated through 3 rounds of internal feedback, and built a clickable prototype in Figma for usability testing.

05
Usability Testing

Ran moderated testing with 12 participants across 2 rounds. Achieved a 92% task completion rate on the revised onboarding flow — up from 38% on the legacy product.

06
Visual Design & Handoff

Designed the final UI with a comprehensive design system: 120+ components, 8 color tokens, and responsive specs for 4 breakpoints. Delivered dev-ready assets via Figma with detailed annotations.

Results

+47% User Activation Rate
-62% Onboarding Drop-off
-45% Support Tickets
3.2 min Avg. Time to Value

Key Learnings

The biggest win wasn't the visual redesign — it was simplifying the information architecture. Removing 75% of navigation items and introducing progressive disclosure transformed a product that felt overwhelming into one that felt intuitive. The lesson: when in doubt, subtract.

Case Study 02

HealWise — Reimagining Telehealth for Accessibility

Designed an accessible telehealth platform that achieved WCAG 2.1 AA compliance and increased appointment completion rates by 35% among users 65+.

RoleSenior UX Designer
Timeline4 months
Team1 Designer, 3 Engineers, 1 PM, 1 Researcher
IndustryHealthcare / Telehealth
HealWise — Patient Dashboard

The Challenge

HealWise's existing telehealth app was failing its most critical demographic: elderly and less tech-savvy patients. The appointment booking flow had a 52% abandonment rate among users over 65, and the video consultation interface generated constant support calls due to confusing controls.

Accessibility was not an afterthought — it was the core design constraint. The platform needed to serve patients with low vision, motor impairments, and limited digital literacy without compromising the experience for other users.

Goals

User Goals
  • Book an appointment without assistance
  • Join a video call confidently
  • Access medical records easily
  • Feel safe and supported throughout
Business Goals
  • Achieve WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
  • Reduce appointment abandonment by 40%
  • Cut support calls by 50%
  • Expand into senior care market

Process

01
Inclusive Research

Partnered with 3 senior care facilities to conduct contextual inquiry sessions. Observed 18 patients using the existing app and documented 47 accessibility barriers across the journey.

02
Accessibility Audit

Performed a comprehensive WCAG 2.1 audit using automated tools and manual screen reader testing. Documented 92 violations and prioritized them by severity and user impact.

03
Simplified Flows

Reduced the booking flow from 7 steps to 3. Introduced a "guided mode" with larger touch targets, clearer labels, and step-by-step audio instructions for users who opted in.

04
Inclusive Testing

Tested with 20 participants across age groups, abilities, and assistive technology setups. Iterated 4 times on the video consultation interface to achieve 95% task completion with screen readers.

Results

+35% Appointment Completion (65+)
100% WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance
-58% Support Calls
4.8/5 Patient Satisfaction Score

Key Learnings

Designing for accessibility improved the experience for everyone — not just the target demographic. The simplified flows, clearer hierarchy, and larger touch targets reduced errors and increased satisfaction scores across all user segments. Inclusive design isn't a constraint; it's a competitive advantage.

Case Study 03

Cartly — Conversion-Driven E-Commerce Redesign

Redesigned the mobile checkout experience for a D2C brand, lifting mobile conversion by 28% and reducing cart abandonment by 41%.

RoleUX/UI Design Lead
Timeline3 months
Team2 Designers, 5 Engineers, 1 PM
IndustryE-Commerce / D2C
Cartly — Mobile Checkout Flow

The Challenge

Cartly, a fast-growing D2C brand, was seeing 73% of their traffic from mobile but converting at only 1.2% — well below the 3% industry benchmark. Analytics revealed that the checkout flow was the primary bottleneck: a 5-page desktop-first design that hadn't been optimized for mobile users.

With $2.4M in monthly revenue at stake, even a small conversion improvement would have a significant bottom-line impact.

Goals

User Goals
  • Complete checkout in under 60 seconds
  • Feel confident about payment security
  • Easily apply discounts and promos
  • Track order status seamlessly
Business Goals
  • Lift mobile conversion to 2.5%+
  • Reduce cart abandonment by 35%
  • Increase average order value by 15%
  • Improve mobile NPS score

Process

01
Data Analysis

Analyzed 6 months of funnel data, heatmaps, and session recordings. Identified 5 critical drop-off points: address form, payment method selection, shipping options, promo code entry, and order confirmation.

02
Competitive Analysis

Benchmarked checkout experiences from 12 best-in-class D2C brands. Documented patterns in single-page checkout, express payment, and trust signal placement.

03
Mobile-First Redesign

Consolidated 5 pages into a single-page checkout with progressive disclosure. Designed thumb-friendly controls, integrated Apple Pay and Google Pay, and added real-time form validation.

04
A/B Testing

Ran a controlled A/B test with 50,000 users over 4 weeks. The redesigned checkout outperformed the control across all metrics, with statistical significance reached at week 2.

Results

+28% Mobile Conversion Rate
-41% Cart Abandonment
+18% Average Order Value
42 sec Avg. Checkout Time

Key Learnings

The single biggest conversion lever was trust — not speed. While the streamlined flow reduced friction, the addition of security badges, real-time validation, and transparent pricing had the largest measurable impact on completion rates. Users don't abandon because checkout is slow; they abandon because they don't feel safe.

Methodology

Design Process

A structured, adaptable framework for turning ambiguity into clarity.

01

Discover

Understand the problem space through user research, stakeholder interviews, data analysis, and competitive benchmarking. Identify unmet needs and opportunity areas.

02

Define

Synthesize research into actionable insights. Frame the problem, define success metrics, map user journeys, and align stakeholders around a shared vision.

03

Ideate

Explore multiple directions through sketching, wireframing, and collaborative workshops. Diverge before converging — the best solutions come from unexpected places.

04

Design

Craft the solution with attention to hierarchy, consistency, and delight. Build design systems, define interaction patterns, and create pixel-perfect deliverables.

05

Validate

Test with real users. Run usability tests, gather feedback, measure against success metrics, and iterate until the experience meets the quality bar.

06

Optimize

Design doesn't end at launch. Monitor analytics, run A/B tests, gather user feedback, and continuously improve the experience based on real-world data.
